title: "A tiny note on tools"
description: "The tool that fits your hand is the one worth keeping."
pubDate: 2026-05-15
tags: ["tools", "rust", "compilers"]
---

I used to chase tools. New editor, new shell, new note-taker every few months.
The dopamine of a fresh setup is real, and it always felt like *this* one was
finally the right one.

It almost never was. What I actually wanted, underneath the churn, was the
feeling of being capable — and switching tools was the cheapest way to fake it.

The fix wasn't picking the "correct" tool. It was committing to *any* tool long
enough to get past the surface and into the part where it starts to disappear.
That's the only useful state. Until then you're not writing code or notes,
you're configuring an environment that will eventually let you write code or
notes.

Pick the thing. Use it for a year. Re-evaluate when something concrete is
actually broken, not when you're bored on a Sunday.
