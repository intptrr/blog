There's a particular kind of clarity you only get when you know someone else
might read what you wrote. Private notes drift. They contradict themselves.
You forgive yourself for hand-waves you'd never let through in public.

Writing in public is a forcing function. The audience doesn't even need to
exist — the *possibility* of a reader is enough to make you check the
load-bearing sentence, look up the date, and admit when you don't know.

## What I try to do

1. **Pick a real question.** If I can't summarize the post as a question and an
   attempted answer, the post isn't ready.
2. **Cut the framing paragraph.** I almost always start with one. It almost
   always belongs in the trash.
3. **Link the source.** If a claim has a source, link it. If it doesn't, say so.

## What I try not to do

- Cargo-cult the form of essays I admire. Cadence is downstream of having
  something to say.
- Pad with examples I'm not sure about, hoping volume = rigor.
- Hedge so hard the post says nothing.

The bar is low: was this post worth the reader's three minutes? Most of mine
aren't. The ones that are make the rest worth writing.
